Career Advice
Expert guidance to help you navigate your career journey.
🎯
Define Your Goals
Know what you want before you apply. Clarity on role, industry, and culture fit saves time and leads to better outcomes.
📊
Research Market Rates
Use platforms like Glassdoor and LinkedIn Salary to benchmark your expectations. Don't undervalue yourself.
🤝
Network Proactively
70% of jobs are filled through referrals. Attend industry events, connect on LinkedIn, and keep relationships warm.
📄
Tailor Every Application
Generic applications rarely succeed. Customise your CV and cover letter for each role you apply to.
💡
Upskill Continuously
Industry trends shift fast. Invest in certifications, online courses, and side projects to stay ahead.
🧘
Mind the Long Game
Career growth is rarely linear. Be patient, stay consistent, and measure progress over years, not months.
Resume Tips
Build a CV that gets you shortlisted.
1
Keep it to 1–2 pages
Recruiters spend 6–7 seconds scanning. Be concise and impactful.
2
Use action verbs
Start bullets with Led, Built, Improved, Reduced, Increased, Delivered.
3
Quantify achievements
"Reduced costs by 22%" beats "helped reduce costs". Numbers stand out.
4
ATS-friendly formatting
Use standard fonts, avoid tables & graphics in the main body. Save as PDF.
5
Tailor for each role
Mirror keywords from the job description to pass automated screening.
6
Contact info at top
Name, phone, email, LinkedIn URL, city. Keep it clean and easy to find.
7
Education goes last
Unless you're a fresher, your experience section is more important.
8
Proofread ruthlessly
Spelling errors kill applications. Use Grammarly or ask a friend to check.
Interview Tips
Walk in confident. Walk out with an offer.
Before the Interview
Research the company, role, and interviewer on LinkedIn. Prepare 5 strong examples using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result).
The First 5 Minutes
Arrive early. Firm handshake, eye contact, smile. First impressions are formed within seconds — make yours count.
Answering Tough Questions
Take a breath before answering. Use STAR. It's okay to say "let me think for a moment." Honesty about gaps beats bluffing.
Asking Smart Questions
Prepare 3–5 thoughtful questions about the team, culture, and success metrics. It shows genuine interest.
Salary Negotiation
Don't mention salary first. When asked, give a range based on market research. Be confident but collaborative.
Following Up
Send a thank-you email within 24 hours. Reference something specific from the conversation. It's rare and memorable.
